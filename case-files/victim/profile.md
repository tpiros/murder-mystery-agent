# Victim Profile: Richard Thornwood

## Personal Information
- **Full Name**: Richard Edward Thornwood
- **Age**: 62
- **Occupation**: Art collector, gallery owner
- **Net Worth**: Approximately $45 million

## Background

Richard Thornwood built his fortune through shrewd art investments and running the prestigious Thornwood Gallery. Known for his keen eye and sometimes ruthless business practices, he had made both friends and enemies in the art world.

## Recent Activities

- Preparing to testify as a key witness in a fraud case involving a former business partner
- Had recently fired his personal assistant of 5 years under unclear circumstances
- Was renegotiating several large debts owed to art dealers
- Recently updated his will, naming his nephew as primary beneficiary

## Personality

Described by associates as brilliant but difficult. Known to hold grudges and had been involved in several lawsuits over the years. However, those close to him noted moments of genuine kindness.

## Estate

Thornwood Manor has been in the family for three generations. The library where he was found contained his most prized private collection, valued at over $12 million.

## Final Day

On the day of his death, Richard had been anxious about the upcoming trial testimony. He had invited select guests for dinner, calling it a "celebration" though none of the guests knew what they were celebrating.
